executive interviews

listening to how leaders actually decide

roleresearcher
method1:1 interviews
outputthemes & frameworks

why

every ai product pitch cites "what executives want." almost none of them actually start from conversations with executives. i wanted to hear, in their own words, how leaders are thinking about ai adoption — where they're excited, where they're stuck, and what they wish builders understood.

what i did

  • conducted a series of in-depth 1:1 interviews with executives across tech, finance, and retail
  • developed an interview protocol that surfaced real workflows rather than buzzwords
  • synthesized findings into recurring themes and tensions — especially the gap between "ai strategy" at the top and tool adoption on the ground

what i learned

the most interesting insights came from the moments executives paused or contradicted themselves. the stated problem ("we need better dashboards") was rarely the real problem ("i don't trust what my team is telling me"). interview craft is mostly about staying in those moments without rushing to the next question.

what it shaped

these conversations directly influenced how i think about agentic product design — especially the trust and accountability gaps that still block adoption, even when the technology is clearly good enough.
