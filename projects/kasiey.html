the problem

founders and executives drown in context-switching — meetings, notes, follow-ups, intro emails, half-remembered commitments. a real human chief of staff is expensive; the work is mostly pattern-matching across a person's own information.

what we built

kasiey is an ai chief of staff that sits on top of your notes, calendar, and inbox. it surfaces what actually needs your attention, drafts the follow-ups you keep forgetting, and remembers context across conversations so you don't have to.

  • agentic task flow using claude with tool-use for calendar, email, and notes
  • memory layer so kasiey builds a long-running picture of who you care about and what you're working on
  • conversational interface — ask "what am i forgetting?" and get a real answer

what i worked on

i focused on the agent's memory and prioritization logic — how it decides what surfaces to the top, and how it avoids becoming another notification stream. also shaped the product voice: kasiey should feel like a thoughtful colleague, not a chatbot.

what i learned

the hard part of an ai chief of staff isn't the model — it's trust. users won't delegate unless they feel confident the agent is seeing the full picture. designing for that confidence became the real product work.
