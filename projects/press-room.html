The Gemini Press Room an automated AI visibility desk

Ten hours. One builder. A competitive intelligence desk that never stops publishing.

The Press Room is a live competitive intelligence dashboard that tracks how AI answer engines — ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, and others — cite and rank brands inside their generated answers. Built solo at Profound's Marketing Engineering Hackathon, it applies traditional competitive intelligence methodology — market share tracking, competitor benchmarking, source attribution — to an entirely new category: answer-engine visibility. Standings, leaks, sourcing, and a drafted response, refreshed from live data.

Why Gemini

In business school, Claude and ChatGPT were the tools everyone defaulted to. Gemini was noticeably underrepresented in that mix, despite Google's scale and distribution advantages — a brand with every structural reason to lead the category, but a competitive position that didn't match. That gap was worth a closer competitive read.

What I Built

A competitive intelligence dashboard with five parts, read in sequence like an analyst's briefing: a TL;DR stating the competitive thesis up front, a Standings table benchmarking the full competitive field by share of voice, a Leaks table quantifying revenue at risk from lost competitive position, an Influence Desk mapping where brand narratives actually get built (community and creator surfaces, not paid media), and a Press Kit translating diagnosis into action — drafted counter-narrative briefs targeted at the exact sources already shaping each underperforming answer.

How the Pipeline Works

Three scripts run in sequence and write to a shared pressroom.json file that the front-end reads on load.

refresh.mjs
Calls Profound's visibility and citations endpoints to pull brand standings, worst-performing prompts, and top cited domains.
revenue_ladder.mjs
For each prompt where Gemini sits below position 3, calculates an inclusion gap and applies a conversion model to produce an annualized revenue-at-risk figure.
diagnose.mjs
Sends each underperforming prompt to the Anthropic Claude API. Claude returns a drafted counter-narrative grounded in the specific sources the engines already cite for that topic.
index.html
Single-file report. Reads static data by default. Live refresh overlays real-time Profound data when an API key is provided — the two modes coexist without a backend.

Revenue Model

The core formula: monthly mentions × inclusion gap × 15% consideration rate × 3% conversion × $240 annual seat value. The inclusion gap normalizes how far outside the top-3 band each prompt sits — a brand at position 13 loses more potential consideration than one at position 4. The output is an annualized revenue exposure figure, clearly labeled as an estimate based on assumed inputs, not disclosed data. This uses an assumed annual contract value of $240 per seat for Gemini — an estimate, not a disclosed or confirmed figure.

Every prompt where Gemini is buried below position 3 has a cost. The model makes that cost visible and sortable — so teams can prioritize by impact, not gut feel.

Key Decisions

01

Revenue at risk is annualized, not monthly. The dollar figure represents the annualized revenue exposure implied by one month of lost competitive visibility, using an assumed $240/seat/year contract value — clearly labeled as an estimate, not a disclosed or confirmed figure.

02

Product-level tracking, not parent-company rollups. "Gemini" and "Google" are tracked as separate competitive entities, as are "Claude"/"Anthropic" and "ChatGPT"/"OpenAI" — a mention of the parent company isn't necessarily a mention of the specific AI product's competitive standing.

03

Caught and fixed a brand-normalization bug post-hackathon. Case-sensitive string keys were splitting single competitors into duplicate rows ("Mistral" vs. "MISTRAL"), silently understating their true market share. Traced to the aggregation logic and fixed at the source — the same data hygiene any competitive analysis depends on.

04

The Press Kit gets its own visual treatment. It's the report's actual competitive recommendation — not another data section — so it's set apart with a distinct dark panel rather than blending into the page's default styling.

05

Integrating a brand-new API with thin documentation. Profound's API is young, and the integration surfaced real friction beyond typical API work — sparse documentation meant more first-principles troubleshooting and direct outreach to confirm behavior that a mature, well-documented API would have made straightforward.

What It Shows

The Press Room is a proof of concept for a new category of brand intelligence: automated AI-answer monitoring. As AI-generated responses displace traditional search results, the brands that win will be the ones who can measure their AI-search presence the way they currently manage SEO — and respond when they lose ground.

AI search isn't a future problem. Brands are already being recommended or ignored by AI answers at scale — and most have no infrastructure to know which, or why, or what to do about it.
